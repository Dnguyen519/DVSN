Wedding Films

01

Vlad & Carmela

Wedding Film 2025
+

Carmela and Vlad's wedding was a truly beautiful experience. The venue itself was stunning, but more than that, the air was thick with love—for the couple, for the families, for the moment. You could feel it in every interaction, every glance, every toast.

One part that had me laughing long after was Carmela's dad and his license plate—I had to include that in the video. It was too good not to.

As always, I felt that familiar nervousness going in, that pressure to create something solid. But Carmela and Vlad made it easy. They were present, relaxed, real—and that helped everything fall into place. Having my sister Judy there, directing the photography, made it even smoother. She's a pro, and we've got a rhythm that just works.

I ended up sticking around longer than planned to film the later parts of the night, and I'm glad I did. The energy shifted, the lights got lower, and it was all just… good vibes. Exactly the kind of footage that brings a story full circle.

Big congrats to Carmela and Vlad—here's to more good times ahead.

Congrats to Dan and Marissa—wishing you both a happy marriage!

04

Devin & Yeelok

Engagement 2024
+

Devin and Yeelok—these two have been my friends for years. I still remember meeting Yeelok for the first time a decade ago, right around when they met each other. It's wild how fast time moves, and even wilder how far they've come together.

Seeing them finally get engaged? No way I was missing that.

Funny enough, I was actually working that day. I dashed out on my break to help shoot—just two hectic hours, camera in hand, adrenaline pumping. But honestly, I'm glad it happened that way. It felt raw, real, and worth it.

Congrats to my besties. You already know I'm rooting for you, always.

07

John & Nancy

Engagement 2019
+

John and Nancy's shoot ended up being one of my favorites—mostly because of how last minute it was.

I was living in Toronto at the time when they hit me up, kind of out of the blue, asking if I could come out to Toronto Island to help shoot. No prep, no planning—just grabbed my gear and went.

Somehow, it all came together. The vibe was relaxed, and John, being his usual goofy self, made it super easy to catch all the genuine smiles and laughs. That energy carried through the whole shoot and made the final video feel real.

Glad I was able to be a part of it.
